About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at Cornell University (Cornell Tech), advised by Prof. Nathan Kallus, and a Department of Energy Computational Science Graduate Fellow (DOE CSGF). I develop machine learning algorithms for causal inference, reinforcement learning, and decision-making under uncertainty. My research tackles challenges in data-driven decision-making, including experiments with imperfect compliance, risk and sensitivity analysis in observational studies, off-policy evaluation, and structured datasets such as spatiotemporal and networked data.

Prior to academia, I was a Senior Data and Applied Scientist at Microsoft Research, where I was a core contributor to EconML, an open-source Python library for causal machine learning. My work helped design and implement widely used methods for estimating treatment effects from observational and experimental data, enabling robust decision-making in industry applications.

My contributions include:

  • Individualized treatment effect estimation from observational data [ICML'19, NeurIPS'19]
  • Risk and sensitivity analysis in observational causal inference studies [AISTATS'23, ICML'23, NeurIPS'24]
  • Experimental data with imperfect compliance [NeurIPS'24, Preprint'25]
  • Spatiotemporal causal inference [Preprint'25]

My goal is to develop trustworthy and effective models for real-world, data-limited, and high-impact settings such as healthcare, climate science, and AI-driven decision-making. If you would like to collaborate on any of these topics and beyond, please reach out!

Bio

Before my PhD, I was a Senior Data and Applied Scientist at Microsoft Research, where I developed data science tools for various applications, including causal inference, cancer research, and weather forecasting. I was a key member of the ALICE team and a core contributor to EconML. During my PhD, I interned at Netflix and Brookhaven National Laboratory. I hold an A.B. in Physics and Mathematics with a minor in Computer Science from Harvard University.

I also serve on the board of the Summer Science Program (SSP), a nonprofit that provides immersive research experiences for exceptional high school students. SSP was a transformative experience for me as an alumna (SSP'09), and I'm thrilled to support its mission and help shape its future.
